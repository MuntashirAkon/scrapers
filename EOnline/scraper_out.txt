<section data-swiftype-index="false" data-textblock-tracking="text-block-video-text"><p><em>Lemonade</em>?</p><p>Pleassssse.</p><p>As soon as<strong><a href="/news/taylor_swift">Taylor Swift </a></strong> <a href="http://www.eonline.com/news/875764/taylor-swift-s-clapback-begins-with-look-what-you-made-me-do-why-reputation-will-be-the-most-merciless-album-of-2017" target="_blank">announced her music comeback</a> Wednesday, many people drew comparisons to the hype and impact of <strong><a href="/news/beyonce">Beyoncé</a></strong>'s 2016 mega hit visual album <em>Lemonade</em>. <section data-swiftype-index="false" data-textblock-tracking="text-block-text-only"><p>The video for "Look What You Made Me Do" is set to be released Sunday during the <strong><a href="/news/2017_mtv_vmas">2017 MTV VMAs</a></strong>. The single is Swift's edgiest single yet, incorporating an electro-pop sound and themes of power and betrayal.</p><p><a href="http://www.eonline.com/news/875793/taylor-swift-s-look-what-you-made-me-do-lyrics-decoded-is-she-calling-out-katy-perry-and-kanye-west" target="_blank">Many speculate</a> it is about <strong><a href="/news/kanye_west">Kanye West</a></strong>. A feud between them began in 2009 after he famously interrupted her VMAs acceptance speech to declare that her award for Best Female Video should have gone to, yep, Beyoncé. They later reconciled, but <a href="http://www.eonline.com/news/875990/taylor-swift-s-album-release-date-has-nothing-to-do-with-kanye-west-s-mother-s-death-anniversary" target="_blank">renewed their beef in 2016</a> after he released the song "Famous," which contains the lyrics, "I feel like me and Taylor might still have sex / Why? I made that bitch famous."</p><p>In the teaser for the video, Swift is seen biting on diamond jewelry in anger while wearing multiple rings, including snake-shaped ones. Her critics have often branded her a snake and she <a href="http://www.eonline.com/news/874778/taylor-swift-shares-a-mysterious-video-after-her-social-media-blackout" target="_blank">used a video of one</a> to tease her announcement about her new single as well as her new album <em>Reputation</em>, which will be released in November.</p><p>Kahn, who directed four of Swift's past music videos from her <em>1989</em> album—"Blank Space," "Bad Blood" "Wildest Dreams" and "Out of the Woods," also defended Swift on Twitter Friday.</p><p>"A young self made woman works hard with talent and intelligence while staying grounded and polite to everyone," he <a href="https://twitter.com/JosephKahn/status/901186813770973184" target="_blank">wrote</a>. "Scandalous."</p><p>Kahn later talked about the making of the "Look What You Made Me Do" video as the finishing touches were put on it.</p><p>"We started concepting and prepping it in January," he <a href="https://twitter.com/JosephKahn/status/901371322332135424" target="_blank">tweeted</a> Saturday morning. "Shot in May. Post production until tomorrow morning. You see it Sssssssunday."</p></section>
