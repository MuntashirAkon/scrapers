Levine also wrote 9 Scorpions, a thriller set at the Supreme Court of the United States. (Published in 1998 by Simon & Schuster, the novel is still in print under the title "Impact").
Levine moved from Florida to Los Angeles in 1999 to accept an invitation from his friend, television producer and fellow Penn State alumnus Don Bellisario, to become a writer on the CBS military series JAG.[10] He wrote 21 teleplays for the series, which aired from 1995 to 2005.[11][better source needed] Levine and Bellisario also co-created the 2002 CBS drama series First Monday, starring Joe Mantegna as a new U.S. Supreme Court justice and James Garner as the chief justice.[12][13]
