The novel was adapted into a silent film directed by Richard Garrick in 1920.[2]
A second silent adaptation was made by Howard Hawks in 1929.[3] The most recent film adaptation of Trent's Last Case was directed in 1952 by Herbert Wilcox. The 1952 film starred Michael Wilding as Trent, Orson Welles as Sigsbee Manderson, and Margaret Lockwood as Margaret Manderson.[4]
An excerpt of the book is recited in the movie Places in the Heart (1984). "Trent's Last Case" by E.C. Bentley. The only part we ever hear is this opening line: "Chapter 1. Bad News. Between what matters and what seems to matter, how shall the world we know judge wisely ..."
